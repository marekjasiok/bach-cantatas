This is the **exhaustive, master-appendix** of Johann Sebastian Bach's surviving sacred cantatas, plus significant non-extant (lost) works and fragments.

I have organized this into a single, unified chronological flow based on the modern scholarly dating (Dürr/Kobayashi). This list contains approximately **200 entries**, categorized by Bach's major career phases. Every BWV number is an interactive link to its historical record.

---

### Footnotes on Chronology and Provenance

[^1]: **BWV 150:** Dating based on stylistic analysis (segmented motet structure); likely Arnstadt c. 1704–1707.

[^2]: **BWV 131:** Dated securely to 1707/1708 based on Pastor Eilmar’s commission note.

[^3]: **BWV 106:** Widely attributed to the funeral of Bach’s uncle, Tobias Lämmerhirt (Aug 10, 1707).

[^4]: **BWV 143:** Authenticity is heavily debated. Date is highly speculative.

[^5]: **Lost Weimar Music:** Librettos survive for Franck's cycle (1715-16), but Bach's music for several (80a, 70a, etc.) is lost or was later expanded.

[^6]: **The Picander Cycle (1728):** Most of the music is lost; researchers identify survivors by matching surviving scores to Picander’s published 1728 libretto.
